It is generally runnable after rain in winter and early spring. It is mainly too low and lots of snakes in the summer. Width, water levels and drainage are similar to Hurricane Creek in Tuscaloosa. The guage is hand painted on the old, no longer used, Hannah Creek Rd bridge. It faces downstream on two river right supports. [PARAGRAPH_BREAK] [PARAGRAPH_BREAK] Each creek is unique in it's own way. Davis' claim to fame is coal mines. It resides smack in the middle of a major coal mine zone. The countryside has been stripped, and it is a bit desolate in some areas. The creek is surprisingly clear though, the strip mining ended some decades ago. Now the mines are subsurface. The coal seams are heavy with methane, so air pumps dot the landscape. These produce noise and sulfur gas, which is a bit stinky.[PARAGRAPH_BREAK] [PARAGRAPH_BREAK] The first two runs are low gradient class I. the scenery is nice but section two has a lot of abandoned tires.
